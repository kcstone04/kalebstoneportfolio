Experience

I had always loved reading stories and coming up with my own in my head. It may sound cliché, but it’s true that I’ve always had one story or another brewing in the depths of my mind. That is how I got into the field of Creative Writing. My dream is to one day be a somewhat successful published author. I want my stories to be read and enjoyed my as many people as possible. On a more personal level, writing and thinking about my stories is entertaining and relaxing for me, so if I could one day do that professionally it would be incredible. I have already taken a few classes in Creative Writing, and have written a small portfolio’s worth of short stories that I am proud of. Currently, I am working on a much larger-scale story that I am aiming to get published in the coming years. In the meantime, I am seeking to publish short stories in various literary journals and magazines.

On the flipside, Professional and Technical Writing (PTW) was my other major in college. I learned about PTW when I first visited Virginia Tech, and it sounded like a fantastic way to complement my other major, being Creative Writing. I took basic HTML when I was in middle school, and I really enjoyed it, which made the web design aspect of PTW a good fit. Furthermore, I have some freelance editing experience, including editing college essays and legal documents. I have experience tutoring writing from working at the Virginia Tech Writing Center, as well as editing experience from my internships with NOEMI Press and the minnesota review. I hope to put all of these skills to good use in the future.
